Festivals of Nepal

About Nepal

Nepal is a landlocked country in South Asia, located in the Himalayas. It is bordered by China to the north and India to the south, east, and west. Nepal is home to eight of the world's fourteen 8,000-meter peaks, including Mount Everest, the highest mountain in the world.

Celebrating Festivals in Nepal

Nepal is known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant festivals. The country celebrates a wide range of festivals throughout the year, reflecting its diverse religious and ethnic communities. One of the most significant festivals is Dashain, which is a Hindu festival that lasts for 15 days and celebrates the victory of the goddess Durga over the demon Mahishasura. During Dashain, families come together, and blessings are sought from elders.

Another prominent festival is Tihar, also known as Deepawali or the Festival of Lights. It is a five-day festival celebrated by Hindus to honor various animals and objects, including crows, dogs, cows, and oxen. Each day of Tihar has its unique rituals and significance, making it a colorful and joyful time in Nepal.

Indra Jatra, celebrated in the Kathmandu Valley, is a Newar festival dedicated to Lord Indra, the god of rain. The festival includes a grand procession of Kumari, the living goddess, and the Kumari Jatra, which showcases traditional dances and rituals.
